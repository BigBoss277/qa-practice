# QA vs QC vs Testing

## Simple Explanation

QA (Quality Assurance) is a process focused on preventing defects and improving the way the product is built. It includes planning, standards, processes, test strategy, and risk prevention.

QC (Quality Control) is a process focused on checking the finished product or feature to find defects and verify quality before release.

Testing is a practical activity within QC. It means executing checks, test cases, exploratory sessions, API requests, or other validations to find problems and confirm that the product works as expected.

## Example From My Project

My three QA practice projects are examples of testing activities, because I checked real features, wrote test cases, created checklists, documented issues, and recorded exploratory notes.

They can also be connected to QC because I evaluated the quality of finished features.

They are not full QA examples by themselves, because QA is broader than testing. QA also includes process improvement, prevention, standards, planning, and team-level quality practices.

## Interview Answer

QA is the broader process of improving and assuring product quality. It focuses on preventing defects and building a good testing and development process.

QC is focused on checking the product and finding defects before release.

Testing is a practical activity inside QC. For example, in my projects I tested login, Google Search, and shopping cart features by writing test cases, checklists, bug reports, and exploratory notes.
