# SDLC vs STLC vs Agile

## Simple Explanation

SDLC (Software Development Life Cycle) is the full process that a team follows to create, release, and maintain software. It includes planning, requirements analysis, design, development, testing, deployment, and maintenance.

STLC (Software Testing Life Cycle) is the testing process inside the broader SDLC. It includes requirement analysis, test planning, test case design, test environment setup, test execution, defect reporting, retesting, regression testing, and test closure.

Agile is a flexible and iterative approach to software development and project management. Instead of building the whole product in one long phase, the team works in short cycles, often called sprints, and regularly delivers small improvements.

## Example From My Project

My three QA practice projects are examples of STLC-related activities because I created checklists, test plans, test cases, bug reports, and exploratory testing notes.

They are not full SDLC examples because I did not participate in requirements analysis, design, development, deployment, or maintenance.

I have not used Agile in a real team yet, but my projects can be connected to Agile-style work because each project focused on a specific feature and produced small, clear testing deliverables.

## Interview Answer

SDLC is the full life cycle of a software product, from planning and requirements to development, testing, release, and maintenance.

STLC is the testing part of that process. It describes how QA work is planned, designed, executed, documented, and closed.

Agile is an iterative way of working where the team builds and tests the product in small increments. In Agile, QA usually works closely with developers and participates throughout the sprint, not only at the end.
